    Exercises 7.3 Exercises

    1.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space, and let \(f\) be a measurable extended real-valued function with measurable domain \(E\text{.}\) Show that the set
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \{x \in E \colon f(x) =c \} \end{equation*}
    is measurable for all \(c \in \mathbb{R}\cup \{-\infty,\infty\}.\) (HINT: you will need to treat the values \(c=-\infty\) and \(c=\infty\) differently than \(c \in \mathbb{R}\)).
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Give an example of a measurable space \((X,\mathcal{F})\) and an extended real-valued function \(f\) with measurable domain \(E\) which satisfies
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \{x \in E \colon f(x) =c \} \end{equation*}
    is measurable for all \(c \in \mathbb{R}\cup \{-\infty,\infty\}\text{,}\) but \(f\) is not a measurable function.
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space. Let \(A \subseteq X\) and let \(f = \chi_A\) be the characteristic function on \(A\text{.}\) Show that \(f\) is a measurable function if and only if \(A\) is a measurable set.
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F},\mu)\) be a measure space which is not complete. Show that there is a measurable function \(f\) on \(X\text{,}\) and a function \(g\) on \(X\) such that \(f =g\) almost everywhere, but \(g\) is not a measurable function.
    🔗
    🔗

    5.

    Let \(f,g\) be two continuous functions on \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Show that if \(f = g\) almost everywhere, then \(f = g\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    6.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F},\mu)\) be a measure space and let \(\mathcal{L}\) be the collection of all extended real-valued functions on \(X\text{.}\) Define a relation \(\sim\) on \(\mathcal{L}\) by
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} f \sim g \text{ if and only if } f=g \text{ a.e.} \end{equation*}
    Show that \(\sim\) is an equivalence relation on \(\mathcal{L}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    7.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space, and let \(f\) be a measurable function with measurable domain \(E \in \mathcal{F}\text{.}\) Show that \(\alpha f\) is a measurable function for all \(\alpha \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    8.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space and let
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \varphi = \sum_{k=1}^n b_i \chi_{F_i} \end{equation*}
    where \(F_1,\ldots,F_n \subseteq X\) are measurable sets, and \(b_1,\ldots,b_n \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Show that there are disjoint measurable sets \(E_1,\ldots,E_m\) and distinct real numbers \(a_1,\ldots,a_m\) such that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \varphi = \sum_{k=1}^m a_k \chi_{E_k}. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    9.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space, and let \((f_n)_n\) be a sequence of measurable functions on \(X\text{.}\) Show that the functions
    🔗
    1. \(\sup_n f_n\text{;}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\inf_n f_n\text{;}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. \(\limsup_n f_n\text{;}\) and
      🔗
      🔗
    4. \(\displaystyle \liminf_n f_n\)
      🔗
      🔗
    are all measurable functions.
    🔗
    🔗

    10.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be a measure space and let \(f \colon X \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a bounded measurable function. Show that, for all \(\varepsilon > 0\) there exist simple functions \(\varphi\) and \(\psi\) such that \(\varphi \leq f \leq \psi\) and \(0 \leq \psi - \varphi < \varepsilon\) on \(X\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    11.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be a measure space and let \(f \colon X \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) be a bounded measurable function. Show that there is a sequence \((\phi_n)_n\) of simple functions with \(\phi_n\leq f\) for all, such that \((\phi_n)\) converges uniformly to \(f\text{.}\) (HINT: Use Exercise Exercise 7.3.10)
    🔗
    🔗

    12.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space. Let \(\overline{\mathbb{R}} =\mathbb{R}\cup \{-\infty,\infty\}\) be the extended real-line and let \(\mathcal{B}(\overline{\mathbb{R}})\) be the Borel sets in \(\overline{\mathbb{R}}\text{,}\) with respect to the topology defined in Exercise Exercise 4.4.6 (see also Exercise Exercise 4.4.7).
    🔗
    Let \(f \colon X \rightarrow \overline{\mathbb{R}}\) be a function. Show that \(f\) is a measurable function if and only if \(f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}\) for all \(B \in \mathcal{B}(\overline{\mathbb{R}})\text{.}\)
    🔗
