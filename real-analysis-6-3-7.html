    Exercises 2.5 Exercises

    1.

    Prove Proposition 2.1.4. That is, show that the operator norm \(\|\cdot\|_\mathrm{op}\) is a norm on the dual space \(V^*\text{,}\) where \(V\) is a normed vector space.
    🔗
    Further show that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \|\varphi\|_{\mathrm{op}} = \inf \{M \colon |\varphi(v)|\leq M\|v\| \text{ for all }v \in V\} \end{equation*}
    for all \(\varphi \in V^*.\)
    🔗
    🔗

    2.

    Prove Theorem 2.2.4 for the case \(p = 1\text{.}\) That is, assuming that \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) is a \(\sigma\)-finite measure space, show that if \(\varphi \in (L^1(X,\mu))^*\) that there is a \(g \in L^\infty(X,\mu)\) with \(\|g\|_\infty = \|\varphi\|_\mathrm{op}\) such that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \varphi(f) = \int_X fg \,d\mu \end{equation*}
    for all \(f \in L^1(X,\mu)\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Let \(X\) be a non-empty set, and let \(c\) be the counting measure on the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(P(X)\) of all subsets of \(X\text{.}\) We will show that \((\ell^1(X))^* \simeq \ell^\infty(X)\) by the same isomorphism used in Theorem 2.2.4.
    🔗
    1. For each \(f \in \ell^1(X)\text{,}\) let \(L_f = \{x \in X \colon f(x) \neq 0\}\text{.}\) Show that \(L_f\) is a countable set, and that
      \begin{equation*} \int_X f\,dc = \sum_{x \in L_f} f(x). \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Take \(g \in \ell^\infty(X)\text{.}\) Define
      \begin{equation*} T_g(f) = \int_X gf\,dc \end{equation*}
      for all \(f \in \ell^1(X)\text{.}\) Show that \(T_g\) is a linear functional on \(\ell^1(X)\) with \(\|T_g\|_\mathrm{op}= \|g\|_\infty\text{.}\) (You should be able to prove this directly without reference to the proof of Lemma 2.2.2).
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Take \(\varphi\in (\ell^1(X))^*\text{.}\) For each \(x \in X\text{,}\) let \(e_x\) be the function
      \begin{equation*} e_x(y) = \begin{cases} 1 &\text{if }y=x\\ 0 & \text{if }y\neq x.\end{cases} \end{equation*}
      Show that \(e_x \in \ell^1(X)\) with \(\|e_x\|_1 = 1\text{,}\) for all \(x\in X\text{.}\)
      🔗
      Define a function \(g\) on \(X\) by \(g(x) = \varphi(e_x)\text{.}\) Show that \(g\in \ell^\infty(X)\text{.}\) Show that \(\varphi = T_g\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Let \(X\) be an uncountable set, and let \(c\) be the counting measure on the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(P(X)\) of all subsets of \(X\text{.}\) Let \(c_0\) be the restriction of \(c\) to the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{M}\) of all subsets of \(X\) which are either countable or cocountable (see Exercise II.2.4.1 for more on \(\mathcal{M}\text{.}\)) We will show that \((L^1(X,c_0))^*\) is not \(L^\infty(X,c_0)\text{.}\) Exercise 2.5.3 should be completed before this exercise.
    🔗
    1. Show that \(L^1(X,c_0)= \ell^1(X)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Show that a simple function \(\varphi\) on \((X,\mathcal{M},c_0)\) must be constant on a cocountable set. Conclude that \(L^\infty(X,c_0) \subsetneq \ell^\infty(X)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Show that the map \(g \mapsto T_g\) is not an isomorphism from \(L^\infty(X,c_0)\) to \((L^1(X,c_0))^*)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    5.

    Denote by \(c_0\) all sequences of real numbers which converges to \(0\text{;}\) and for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{.}\) Note that \(c_0 \subseteq \ell^\infty\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. Show that \(c_0\) is a Banach space with the \(\infty\)-norm.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Show that the collection of sequences which are \(0\) for all but finitely many terms are dense in \(c_0\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Show that \(c_0\) is separable.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    6.

    Denote by \(c_0\) all sequences of real numbers which converges to \(0\text{;}\) and for each \(n \in \mathbb{N}\text{,}\) denote by \(e_n\) the sequence which is \(1\) in the \(n\)th position, and \(0\) in all other positions.
    🔗
    1. Take \(a = (a_n)_n \in \ell^1\text{.}\) Show that the map
      \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} T_a \colon & c_0 \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \\ & (b_n)_n \mapsto \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_nb_n \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
      defines a linear functional on \(c_0\text{,}\) with \(\|T_a\|_\mathrm{op} = \|a\|_1\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Take \(\varphi \in (c_0)^*\text{.}\) For each \(n\text{,}\) let \(a_n = \varphi(e_n)\text{.}\) Show that \((a_n)_n \in \ell^1\) and that \(\varphi = T_a\text{.}\) (HINT: consider \(\varphi(\sum_{n=1}^N \gamma_n e_n)\) where \(\gamma_n = \operatorname{sgn}(\varphi(e_n))\)).
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Show that the map \(T \colon a \mapsto T_a\) is an isometric linear bijection from \(\ell^1\) to \((c_0)^*\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    7.

    1. Show that, if \(a = (a_n)_n \in \ell^1\text{,}\) then the map \(T_a\) defined by
      \begin{equation*} T_a((b_n)_n) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n b_n \end{equation*}
      for all \((b_n)_n \in \ell^\infty\) is a linear functional on \(\ell^\infty\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Take any \(\varphi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\text{.}\) For each \(n\text{,}\) let \(a_n = \varphi(e_n)\text{,}\) where \(e_n\) is defined as in Exercise 2.5.6. Show that \(a=(a_n)_n \in \ell^1\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Take \(\varphi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\) and let \(a=(a_n)_n\) be as in part (ii). Show that \(\varphi(b) = T_a(b)\) for all \(b \in c_0\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Show that if \(\varphi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\text{,}\) then there is an \(a \in \ell^1\) and a \(\psi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\) with \(\psi|_{c_0}=0\) such that
      \begin{equation*} \varphi = T_a + \psi. \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗

    8.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{M})\) be a measurable space. A finitely additive signed measure on \((X,\mathcal{M})\) is a function \(\nu \colon \mathcal{M}\rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) such that
    🔗
    1. \(\nu(\emptyset)=0\text{;}\) and
      🔗
      🔗
    2. \(\nu\left(\bigcup_{k=1}^n E_k\right) = \sum_{k=1}^n \nu(E_k)\) when \(\{E_k\}_{k=1}^n\) are disjoint sets in \(\mathcal{M}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    A finitely additive signed measure is bounded if
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} |\nu|(X) := \sup\left\{\sum_{k=1}^n \nu(E_k) \colon \{E_k\}_{k=1}^n\subseteq\mathcal{M} \text{ disjoint sets.}\}\right\}<\infty. \end{equation*}
    1. Show that the collection of all bounded finitely additive signed measures on \((X,\mathcal{M})\) forms a vector space.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. If \(\nu\) is a bounded finitely additive signed measure, let \(\|\nu\|_\mathrm{var} = |\nu|(X)\text{.}\) Show that \(\|\cdot\|_\mathrm{var}\) is a norm on the vector space of bounded finitely additive signed measures on \((X,\mathcal{M})\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    9.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{M})\) be a measurable space and let \(\nu\) be a bounded finitely additive signed measure on \((X,\mathcal{M})\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. Let \(\varphi = \sum_{k=1}^n c_k \chi_{E_k}\) be a simple function, where \(c_1,\ldots,c_n\) are distinct, and \(E_1,\ldots,E_n\) are disjoint. Define \(\int_X \varphi \,d\mu\) by
      \begin{equation*} \int_X \varphi\,d\nu = \sum_{k=1}^n c_k \nu(E_k). \end{equation*}
      Show that
      🔗
      \begin{equation*} \left| \int_X \varphi\,d\nu\right| \leq \|\nu\|_{\mathrm{var}} \|\varphi\|_\infty. \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Let \(f\) be a bounded, measurable function on \((X,\mathcal{M})\text{.}\) Let \((\phi_n)_n\) be a sequence of simple functions with \(\phi_n \leq f\) for all \(n\text{,}\) which converge uniformly to \(f\) (Such a sequence exists by Exercise II.5.3.11). Define \(\int_X f\,d\nu\) by
      \begin{equation*} \int_X f\,d\nu = \lim_{n\rightarrow \infty} \int_X \varphi\,d\nu. \end{equation*}
      Show that \(\int_X f\,d\nu\) is independent on the choice of sequence \((\phi_n)_n\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    10.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be a measure space and let \(\mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be the normed vector space of all bounded finitely additive signed measures on \((X,\mathcal{M})\) which are absolutely continuous with respect to \(\mu\text{.}\) That is, \(\mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) is the collection of all bounded finitely additive signed measures \(\nu\) such that \(\nu(E) = 0\) whenever \(\mu(E)=0\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. Show that
      \begin{equation*} \left| \int_X f\,d\nu \right| \leq \|\nu\|_\mathrm{var} \|f\|_\infty \end{equation*}
      for all \(f \in L^\infty(X,\mu)\) and \(\nu \in \mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    2. For \(\nu \in \mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\text{,}\) define \(T_\nu \colon L^\infty(X,\mu) \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) by
      \begin{equation*} T_\nu(f) = \int_X f\,d\nu. \end{equation*}
      Show that \(T_\nu\) is a bounded linear functional on \(L^\infty(X,\mu)\) such that \(\|T_\nu\|_\mathrm{op} = \|\nu\|_\mathrm{var}.\)
      🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Let \(\varphi\) be a bounded linear functional on \(L^\infty(X,\mu)\text{.}\) Define \(\nu\) by \(\nu(E) = \varphi(\chi_E)\) for all \(E \in \mathcal{M}\text{.}\) Show that \(\nu \in \mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) and that \(\|\nu\|_\mathrm{var} = \|\varphi\|_\mathrm{op}.\)
      🔗
      🔗
    4. Show that the map \(T \colon \mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu) \rightarrow (L^\infty(X,\mu))^*\) defined by \(T(\nu) = T_\nu\) is a bijective linear map with \(\|T(\nu)\|_\mathrm{op}= \|\nu\|_\mathrm{var}.\) That is, conclude \(\mathsf{BFA}(X,\mathcal{M},\mu) \simeq (L^\infty(X,\mu))^*.\)
      🔗
      🔗
    The above result is known as the Kantorovitch Representation Theorem.
    🔗
    🔗

    11.

    Prove Proposition 2.3.1. That is, show that \(X\) is compact, if and only if whenever \(\{F_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}\) is a collection of closed sets satisfying \(\bigcap_{k=1}^nF_k \neq \emptyset\) for any \(F_1,\ldots,F_n \in \{F_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}\text{,}\) then
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \bigcap_{\alpha\in A}F_\alpha \neq \emptyset. \end{equation*}
    (HINT: Note that \(\bigcap_{\alpha\in A}F_\alpha \neq \emptyset = \emptyset\) if and only if \(\{X \backslash F_\alpha\}_{\alpha \in A}\) is an open cover of \(X\text{.}\))
    🔗
    🔗

    🔗

    15.

    Let \(X\) be a locally compact Hausdorff space and let \(\mathcal{M}(X)\) be all finite Radon signed measures on \(X\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. Show that \(\mathcal{M}(X)\) is a vector space under the operations
      \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} (\nu + \mu)(E)&= \nu(E) + \mu(E)\text{ and}\\ (c\mu)(E) &= c \mu(E), \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
      for \(\mu,\nu \in \mathcal{M}(X)\) and \(c \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
