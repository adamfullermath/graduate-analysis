    Exercises 9.5 Exercises

    1.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space and let \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) be two measures on \((X,\mathcal{F})\text{.}\) Take any non-negative measurable function on \((X,\mathcal{F})\text{.}\) Show that, for all \(E \in \mathcal{F}\)
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \int_E f \,d(\mu+\nu) = \int_E f\,d\mu + \int_E f\,d\nu. \end{equation*}
    🔗

    2.

    Let \((\mathbb{R},\mathcal{M})\) be the measurable space of Lebesgue measurable subsets of \(\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) let \(m\) be the Lebesgue measure on \((\mathbb{R},\mathcal{M})\text{,}\) and let \(c\) be the counting measure on \((\mathbb{R},\mathcal{M})\text{.}\)
    🔗
    1. Show that \(m \ll c\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Show that there does not exist a measurable function \(f\) on \((\mathbb{R},\mathcal{M})\) so that
      \begin{equation*} m(E) = \int_E f\,dc. \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Why does this not contradict the Radon-Nikodym Theorem?
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    3.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F},\mu)\) be a \(\sigma\)-finite measure space and let \(\nu\) be a \(\sigma\)-finite measure on \((X,\mathcal{F})\) with \(\nu \ll \mu\text{.}\) Let \(f = \frac{d\nu}{d\mu}\) be the Radon-Nikodym derivative of \(\nu\) with respect to \(\mu\text{.}\) Let \(E \subseteq \mathcal{F}\) be a measurable set, and let \(g\) be a measurable function on \(X\) which is integrable with respect to \(\nu\text{.}\) Show that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \int_X g\,d\nu = \int_X gf\,d\mu. \end{equation*}
    (HINT: First show the result holds for simple functions)
    🔗
    🔗

    4.

    Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable set. Show that absolute continuity \(\ll\) defines a preorder on the set of all measures on \((X,\mathcal{F})\text{.}\) That is, show
    🔗
    1. \(\mu \ll \mu\) for all measures \(\mu\) (\(\ll\) is symmetric); and
      🔗
      🔗
    2. if \(\mu \ll \nu\) and \(\nu \ll \lambda\text{,}\) then \(\mu \ll \lambda\) (\(\ll\) is transitive).
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    5.

    In Exercise Exercise 9.5.4 it is shown that \(\ll\) is a preorder.
    🔗
    1. Show that \(\ll\) is not a partial order. I.e. show that \(\ll\) is not antisymmetric. That is, show that we can have \(\mu \ll \nu\) and \(\nu \ll \mu\) but \(\mu \neq \nu\text{.}\)
      🔗
      🔗
    2. For \(\sigma\)-finite measures, show that if \(\mu \ll \nu\) and \(\nu \ll \mu\) then there is a scalar \(c\) such that \(\mu = c \nu\text{.}\) That is, let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space and let \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) be non-zero \(\sigma\)-finite measures on \((X,\mathcal{F})\) such that \(\nu \ll \mu\) and \(\mu \ll \nu\text{.}\) Show that the Radon-Nikodym derivatives \(\frac{d\mu}{d\nu}\) and \(\frac{d\nu}{d\mu}\) are constant and (multiplicative) inverses of eachother. (HINT: Recall Exercise Exercise 8.4.7 and Exercise Exercise 9.5.3).
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗

    6.

    Let \(f\) be a continuous function on a closed interval \([a,b]\subseteq\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Show that if \(f'(x)\) exists for all \(x\) and \(f'\) is bounded, then \(f\) is absolutely continuous. (HINT: Recall the Mean Value Theorem).
    🔗
    🔗

    7.

    Complete the proof of Proposition Proposition 9.4.11. That is, if \(A=\{x_n\}_{n=1}^\infty \subseteq [a,b]\) is a countable set, \(\sum_n a_n\) is a convergent series of positive numbers, and \(f\) is the function on \([a,b]\)
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} f(x) = \sum_{n\colon x_n\leq x}a_n, \end{equation*}
    then \(f\) is continuous for all \(x \in [a,b]\backslash A\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗

    8.

    Let \([a,b]\subseteq \mathbb{R}\) be a closed interval, and let \(\{x_1,x_2,\ldots\}\) be a countable subset of \([a,b]\text{.}\) Show that, for real numbers \((a_n)_n\text{,}\)
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} \nu = \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\delta_{x_n} \end{equation*}
    is a Borel signed-measure on \([a,b]\) if and only if \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) converges absolutely.
    🔗
