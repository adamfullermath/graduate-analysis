Example 11.2.6.

Let \(c_0\) be the collection of all sequences of real numbers that converge to \(0\text{.}\) In Exercise Exercise 11.5.5 we establish that \(c_0\) is a separable Banach subspace for \(\ell^\infty\text{.}\) For \(a = (a_n)_n \in \ell^1\text{,}\) we define a linear functional \(T_a\) on \(c_0\) by
🔗
\begin{equation*} T_a((b_n)_n) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_nb_n. \end{equation*}
Exercise Exercise 11.5.6 shows that the map
🔗
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} T \colon & \ell^1 \rightarrow (c_0)^* \\ & a \mapsto T_a \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
is an isometric bijection. That is, \((c_0)^* \simeq \ell^1\text{.}\)
🔗
In Exercise Exercise 11.5.7 we show that if \(\varphi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\) then there is an \(a \in \ell^1\) and a \(\psi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\) with \(\psi(b) = 0\) for all \(b \in c_0\) such that
🔗
\begin{equation*} \varphi = T_a + \psi. \end{equation*}
It is reasonable to believe that there are non-zero elements \(\psi \in (\ell^\infty)^*\) with \(\psi|_{c_0} = 0\text{.}\) After all, \(\ell^\infty\) is a not separable by Theorem Theorem 10.3.13, and \(c_0\) is a separable by Exercise Exercise 11.5.5. That \(\ell^\infty\) is larger than \(c_0\) suggests that \((\ell^\infty)^*\) should be larger than \((c_0)^*\text{.}\) Exhibiting \(\psi\) with this property is between difficult and impossible. However, with the Hahn-Banach theorem, a significant theorem in the field of Functional Analysis, one can show that such \(\psi\) exist. Thus \((\ell^\infty)^*\) is not the same as \(\ell^1\) under the standard isomorphism \(T\text{.}\)
