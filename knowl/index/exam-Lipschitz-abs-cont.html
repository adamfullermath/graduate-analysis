Example 7.2.4.

Let \(f\) be a Lipschitz function on \([a,b]\). That is, assume there is a constant \(K\) such that \(|f(x) - f(y)|\leq K|x-y|\) for all \(x,y\in[a,b]\text{.}\) Take any \(\varepsilon>0\) and let \(\delta = \varepsilon/K\text{.}\) Let \(\{(a_i,b_i)\}_{i=1}^n\) be a collection of disjoint open intervals such that \(\sum_i(b_i-a_i)<\delta\text{.}\) Then
🔗
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \sum_{i=1}^n|f(b_i) - f(a_i)| & \leq\sum_{i=1}^nK(b_i - a_i) \\ &< \varepsilon. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Thus, \(f\) is absolutely continuous.
🔗
Assume now that \(f =0\text{.}\) Let \(\nu\) be the Borel signed-measure on \([a,b]\) induced by \(f\text{,}\) from Theorem 4.2.14. That is, if \(f = f_1 - f_2\) is the Jordan decomposition of \(f\text{,}\) then \(\nu = \nu_+ - \nu_-\) where \(\nu_+\) is the Carathéodory extension of the premeasure \(\nu_+((c,d]) = f_1(d) - f_1(c)\) and \(\nu_-\) is the Carathéodory extension of the premeasure \(\nu_-((c,d]) = f_2(d) - f_2(c)\text{,}\) for \((c,d] \subseteq [a,b]\text{.}\) Following a similar argument as in Example 7.1.5, we can show that \(\nu\) is absolutely continuous.
