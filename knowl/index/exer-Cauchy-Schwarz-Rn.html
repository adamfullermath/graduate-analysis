Exercise 1.

Let \(u = (u_1,\ldots,u_n)\) and \(v = (v_1,\ldots,v_n)\) be two vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) and let \(p\) be the polynomial
🔗
\begin{equation*} p(x) = \left(\sum_{i=1}^n u_i^2\right)x^2 + 2 \sum_{i=1}^n u_iv_i x + \sum_{i=1}^n v_i^2. \end{equation*}
  1. Show that \(p(x)\geq 0\) for all \(x \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)(Hint: write \(p(x)\) as a sum of \(n\) squares).
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Use (i) to prove the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality. That is, show that,
    \begin{equation*} \left(\sum_{i=1}^n u_iv_i \right)^2 \leq \left(\sum_{i=1}^n u_i^2\right)^2\left(\sum_{i=1}^n v_i^2\right)^2. \end{equation*}
    (Hint: What does (i) tell us about the descriminant of \(p\text{?}\))
