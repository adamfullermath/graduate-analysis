Theorem 2.2.3.

Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) be a measure space. Define \(\mathcal{M}_0\) to be the collection of subset of \(X\)
🔗
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{M}_0 = \{A \cup B \colon A \in \mathcal{M},\ B \subseteq C\text{ for some }C \in \mathcal{M} \text{ with }\mu(C) = 0\}. \end{equation*}
For \(E = A \cup B\) in \(\mathcal{M}_0\) where \(A \in \mathcal{M}\) and \(B \subseteq C\in \mathcal{M}\) with \(\mu(C)=0\text{,}\) define \(\mu_0(A\cup B) = \mu(A)\text{.}\) Then \(\mathcal{M}_0\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra and \((X,\mathcal{M}_0,\mu_0)\) is a complete measure space.
