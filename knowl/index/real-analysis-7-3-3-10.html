Example 2.1.6.

The general linear group (of degree \(n\)) \(GL_n(\bR)\text{,}\) is the set of all \(n\times n\) invertible matrices. The general linear group is a group with the operation of matrix multiplication. There are several natural topologies on \(GL_n(\bR)\text{.}\) The group \(GL_n(\bR)\) is a subset of \(M_{n\times n}(\bR)\text{,}\) the collection of all \(n \times n\) matrices. As a vector space, \(M_{n\times n}(\bR)\) is isomorphic to \(\bR^{n^2}\text{.}\) We can thus view \(GL_n(\bR)\) as a subset of \(\bR^{n^2}\text{,}\) and endow \(GL_n(\bR)\) with the relative topology. The general linear group \(GL_n(\bR)\) is a topological group under this topology.
🔗
Another way to get a topology on \(GL_n(\bR)\) is to give the elements of \(M_{n\times n}(\bR)\) the operator norm as defined in Remark 1.3.2. The general linear group \(GL_n(\bR)\) is also a topological group with this topology, see Exercise 2.3.4.
