Let \(\mu \colon \mathcal{S} \rightarrow [0,\infty]\text{.}\) As any measure is a premeasure, in necessary condition for the Carathéodory measure induced by \(\mu\) to agree with \(\mu\) on \(\mathcal{S}\) is for \(\mu\) to be a premeasure. With some additional structure on \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) we can show that the Carathéodory measure is an extension of a premeasure. When this happens, we call the Carathéodory measure a Carathéodory extension.
 1 
It may be the case that there are more measures than the Carathéodory extension which extend a premeasure \(\mu\text{.}\) However, we will see in Theorem 2.3.9 that Carathéodory is sometimes unique.
