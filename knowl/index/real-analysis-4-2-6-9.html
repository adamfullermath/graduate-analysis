Remark 1.4.2.

The axiom of countable choice states that any countable collection of non-empty sets has a choice function. This is a weaker axiom than the axiom of choice. As most functional analysis is carried out with underlying field \(\mathbb{C}\) or \(\mathbb{R}\text{,}\) which contain countable dense subsets (\(\mathbb{Q}\) and \(\mathbb{Q}+ \mathbb{Q} i\) respectively), there are many times that the countable axiom of choice can be used in place of the axiom of choice. However, this leads to more technical proofs in an already technical field, so we will speak no more of the countable axiom of choice. It is also worth noting that (uncountable) choice is needed to show the existence of non-measurable sets.
