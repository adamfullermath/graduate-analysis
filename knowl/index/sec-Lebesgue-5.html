Definition 5.1.2.

The function \(m^* \colon \mathcal{P}(\mathbb{R}) \rightarrow [0,\infty]\) defined by
🔗
\begin{equation*} m^*(A) = \inf \left\{ \sum_{k=1}^\infty \ell(I_k) \colon A \subseteq \bigcup_{k=1}^\infty I_k,\ \{I_k\}_{k=1}^\infty \text{ finite length open intervals}\right\} \end{equation*}
is call the Lebesgue outer measure on \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
🔗
The elements of the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{M}\) of \(m^*\)-measurable sets are called Lebesgue measurable sets.
🔗
The Carathéodory measure \(m\) got by restricting \(m^*\) to \(\mathcal{M}\) is called the Lebesgue measure on \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
