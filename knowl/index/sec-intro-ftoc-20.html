Lebesgue also worked on the theory of differentiation, and its relationship to (both Riemann and Lebesgue) integration. Lebesgue showed that every increasing function is differentiable almost everywhere. This, in turn, can be used to show that a class of continuous functions called absolutely continuous functions, are differentiable almost everywhere. The term `almost everywhere’ is not a vague colloquialism; it has an exact technical definition that we will see later in this course. In Chapter Chapter 2 we will study measure spaces.
