Examples~\ref{exam: popcorn}~and~\ref{exam: char Q} show that Riemann integration can sometimes work well with countable dense subsets, but not always. Hence Riemann integration may not work well when dealing with the derivative of Schwarz’s example. Vito Volterra (1860 - 1940) gave an example in 1881 which gives a further complication to the theory. Volterra’s function \(V \colon [0,1] \rightarrow \mathbb{R}\) is continuous, with \(V'(x)\) bounded and defined for all \(x \in (0,1)\text{.}\) However, \(V'(x)\) is not Riemann integrable. Hence, we can not apply Theorem 1.1.1 to \(V\text{.}\)
