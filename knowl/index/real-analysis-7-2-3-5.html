Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{N},\nu)\) be two measure spaces, and let \((\mu\times\nu)(A\times B)= \mu(A)\nu(B)\) for \(A\in \mathcal{M}\) and \(B \in \mathcal{N}\text{.}\) By Proposition 1.1.2, \(\mu\times\nu\) is a premeasure on a semiring. Thus, by the Carathéodory-Hahn Theorem (Theorem II.2.3.16), \(\mu\times\nu\) extends to a measure on \((\mu\times\nu)^*\)-measurable sets in \(X\times Y\) (where \((\mu\times\nu)^*\) is the outer measure induced by the premeasure \(\mu\times\nu\)). We denote this extension by \(\mu \times \nu\text{.}\) The measure \(\mu\times \nu\) is called the product measure of \(\mu\) and \(\nu\text{.}\)
