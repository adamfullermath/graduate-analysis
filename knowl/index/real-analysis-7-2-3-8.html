Definition 1.1.5.

Let \(X\) and \(Y\) be non-empty sets and let \(E \subseteq X\times Y\text{.}\) For \(x \in X\) the \(x\)-section \(E_x\) of \(E\) is defined as
\begin{equation*} E_x = \{y \in Y \colon (x,y) \in E \}. \end{equation*}
For \(y \in Y\) the \(y\)-section \(E^y\) of \(E\) is defined as
\begin{equation*} E^y = \{x \in X \colon (x,y) \in E\}. \end{equation*}
If \(f\) is a function on \(X\times Y\text{,}\) and \(x\in X\) then the \(x\)-section \(f_x\) of \(f\) is a function on \(Y\) defined by
\begin{equation*} f_x(y)= f(x,y). \end{equation*}
If \(y\in Y\) then the \(y\)-section \(f^y\) of \(f\) is a function on \(X\) defined by
\begin{equation*} f^y(x)= f(x,y). \end{equation*}
