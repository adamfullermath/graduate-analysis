Theorem 1.1.13. The Fubini-Tonelli Theorem.

\label{thm: fubini-tonelli} Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{N},\nu)\) be \(\sigma\)-finite complete measure spaces, and let \(f\) be a \((\mu\times\nu)\)-measurable function on \(X\times Y\text{.}\) Then
🔗
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{X\times Y}{|f|}\,d{(\mu\times\nu)} &= \int_{X}{\left(\intd{Y}\,d{|f(x,y)|}{\nu(y)}\right)}{\mu(x)}\\ &= \int_{Y}{\left(\intd{X}\,d{|f(x,y)|}{\mu(x)}\right)}{\nu(y)}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Further, if any of the three integrals above are finite, then \(f\) is integrable and
🔗
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \int_{X\times Y}{f}\,d{(\mu\times\nu)} &= \int_{X}{\left(\intd{Y}\,d{f(x,y)}{\nu(y)}\right)}{\mu(x)}\\ &= \int_{Y}{\left(\intd{X}\,d{f(x,y)}{\mu(x)}\right)}{\nu(y)}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
