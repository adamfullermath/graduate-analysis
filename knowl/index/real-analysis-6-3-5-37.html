Definition 11.3.12.

Let \(X\) be a locally compact Hausdorff space and let \(\mu\) be a Borel measure on \((X,\mathcal{B}(X))\text{.}\) Take \(E \in \mathcal{B}(X)\text{.}\) The measure \(\mu\) is outer regular on \(E\) if
🔗
\begin{equation*} \mu(E) = \inf\{\mu(U) \colon E \subseteq U,\ U \text{ open}\}, \end{equation*}
and inner regular on \(E\) if
🔗
\begin{equation*} \mu(E) = \sup\{\mu(K) \colon K \subseteq E,\ K \text{ compact}\}. \end{equation*}
The measure \(\mu\) is a Radon measure if \(\mu\) is outer regular on all Borel sets, and inner regular on all open sets.
🔗
A signed Borel measure \(\nu\) on \((X,\mathcal{B}(X))\) is a Radon signed measure if the measure of total variation \(|\nu|\) is a Radon measure.
