By Proposition 1.1.15, we can thus extend \(\mu_1\times\ldots\times \mu_n\) to a measure on \(\prod_{k=1}^n X_k\) by the Carathéodory-Hahn Theorem. This measure is also called the product measure. Just as in the \(n=2\) case, it is not guaranteed that the measure \(\mu_1\times\ldots\times \mu_n\) is the unique extension of the its restriction to the measurable rectangles. However, when each measure space \((X_k,\mathcal{M}_k,\mu_k)\) is a \(\sigma\)-finite measure space, for \(1\leq k \leq n\text{,}\) then we do have uniqueness.
