Proposition 11.3.8. Urysohn’s Lemma.

Let \(X\) be a locally compact Hausdorff space. Let \(K \subseteq X\) be compact and \(U \subseteq X\) be open such that \(K \subseteq U \subseteq X\text{.}\) There exists a continuous, compactly supported function \(f \colon X \rightarrow [0,1]\) such that \(f(x) = 1\) for all \(x \in K\) and \(f(x) = 0\) for all \(x \in X \backslash U\text{.}\)
