Theorem 2.3.9.

Let \(X\) be a non-empty set. Let \(\mathcal{S} \subseteq \mathcal{P}(X)\) be a non-empty collection of subseteq which is closed under relative complements\index{relative complement}. That is, if \(A,B \in \mathcal{S}\text{,}\) then \(A \backslash B \in \mathcal{S}\text{.}\) Let \(\mu \colon \mathcal{S} \rightarrow [0,\infty]\) be a premeasure, and let \(\overline{\mu}\) be the Carath\’{e}odory measure induced by \(\mu\text{.}\) Then each \(A \in \mathcal{S}\) is \(\overline{\mu}\)-measurable, and \(\overline{\mu}(A) = \mu(A)\text{.}\) That is, \(\overline{\mu}\) is the Carath\’{e}odory extenion on \(\mu\text{.}\)
