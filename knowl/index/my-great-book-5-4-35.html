Theorem 2.3.16.

[Carath\’{e}odory-Hahn Theorem]\index{Carath\’{e}odory-Hahn Theorem}\index{Carath\’{e}odory extension} Let \(\mu_0\) be a premeasure on a semiring \(\mathcal{S}\) of subsets of a non-empty set \(X\text{.}\) Then the Carath\’{e}odory measure \(\mu\) induced by \(\mu_0\) is an extension of \(\mu_0\text{.}\) Furthermore, if \(\mu_0\) is \(\sigma\)-finite, then \(\mu\) is \(\sigma\)-finite and \(\mu\) is the unique measure on the set of \(\mu^*\)-measurable subsets extending \(\mu_0\text{.}\)
