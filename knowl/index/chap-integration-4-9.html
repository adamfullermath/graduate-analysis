Theorem 5.2.3. Linearity and Monotonicity of Integration.

\label{thm: int liner} Let \((X,\mathcal{F},\mu)\) be a measure space, let \(f\) and \(g\) be integrable functions on \(X\text{,}\) and let \(\alpha, \beta \in\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Then \(\alpha f + \beta g\) is integrable and
🔗
\begin{equation*} \int_X (\alpha f + \beta g)\,d\mu = \alpha \int_X f\,d\mu + \beta \int_X g\, d\mu. \end{equation*}
Further, it \(f \leq g\) then
🔗
\begin{equation*} \int_X f\, d\mu \leq \int_X g\, d\mu. \end{equation*}
