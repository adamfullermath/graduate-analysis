Example 2.3.12.

Let \((X,\mathcal{M}, \mu)\) and \((Y, \mathcal{N}, \nu)\) be two measure spaces. Let \(\mathcal{S}\) be the collection of subsets of \(X \times Y\) given by
🔗
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{S} = \{ A \times B \colon A \in \mathcal{M}, \ B \in \mathcal{N}\}. \end{equation*}
Then \(\mathcal{S}\) is a semiring. The elements of \(\mathcal{S}\) are called measurable rectangles. More on this later, when we look at product measures.
