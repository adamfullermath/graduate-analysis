Let \(G\) be a topological group, and let \(A,B \subseteq G\text{,}\) and \(x \in G\text{.}\) We define the following pieces of notation:
\begin{align*} x A \amp = \{xa \colon a \in A \} \\ Ax \amp = \{ax \colon a \in A \} \\ AB \amp = \{ab \colon a \in A,\ b \in B \} \text{ and }\\ A^{-1} \amp = \{a^{-1} \colon a \in A \}. \end{align*}
If \(A = A^{-1}\text{,}\) then we say that \(A\) is symmetric.
