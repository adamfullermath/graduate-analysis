Definition 6.1.12.

Let \((X,\mathcal{F},\mu)\) be a \(\sigma\)-finite measure space, let \(\nu\) be a \(\sigma\)-finite measure or a finite signed-measure, and let \(\nu_s\) and \(\nu_a\) are mutually orthogonal measures such that \(\nu_s \perp \mu\text{,}\) \(\nu_a \ll \mu\text{,}\) and \(\nu = \nu_s + \nu_a\text{.}\) The measures \(\nu_s\) and \(\nu_a\) are called the Lebesgue decomposition of \(\nu\) with respect to \(\mu\). We call the Radon-Nikodym derivative of \(\nu_a\) witrh respect to \(\mu\) the Radon-Nikodym derivative on \(\nu\) with respect to \(\mu\) and denote the it by \(\frac{d\nu}{d\mu}\text{.}\)
