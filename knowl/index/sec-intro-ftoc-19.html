These failures of the Riemann integral necessitate defining a new type of integral: the Lebesgue integral. Henri Lebesgue (1875 - 1941) introduced this more robust way integrating functions. For Riemann integrable functions, the Lebesgue integral and the Riemann integral coincide. However, the Lebesgue integral allows us to integrate many functions which are not Riemann integrable, including \(\chi_A\) from Example 1.1.7 and the derivative of Volterra’s function \(V'(x)\text{.}\)
