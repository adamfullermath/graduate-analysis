Definition 2.3.10.

Let \(X\) be a non-empty set and let \(\mathcal{S}\) be a non-empty collection of subsets of \(X\text{.}\) The collection \(\mathcal{S}\) is a semiring (of subsets of \(X\)) if for each \(A,B \in \mathcal{S}\)
🔗
  1. \(A \cap B \in \mathcal{S}\text{;}\) and
    🔗
    🔗
  2. there exists a finite disjoint collection \(\{C_k\}_{k=1}^n\) of sets in \(\mathcal{S}\) such that
    \begin{equation*} A \backslash B = \bigcup_{k=1}^n C_k. \end{equation*}
