\begin{align*} f \colon \amp [0,2\pi) \rightarrow \bT \\ \amp \theta \mapsto e^{i\theta}. \end{align*}
and let \(\mu(B) = m(f^{-1}(B))\) for Borel sets \(B\subseteq \bT\) be as described in Example 2.2.5. We will show that \(\mu\) is a Haar measure on \(\bT\text{.}\)
