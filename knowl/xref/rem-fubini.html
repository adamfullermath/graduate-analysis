Remark 1.1.11.

Let \(f\) be a measurable function on a product space \(X\times Y\text{.}\) Note that, in order to apply Fubini’s theorem, we must first know that \(f\) is integrable with repsect to the product measure \(\mu \times \nu\text{.}\) It is possible that the iterated integral
\begin{equation*} \int_X \left( \int_Y f(x,y)\,d\nu(y)\right) \,d\mu(x) \end{equation*}
exists and is finite, but \(f\) itself is not integral with resepct to \(\mu \times \nu\text{.}\) An example is given in Exercise 1.4.5.
