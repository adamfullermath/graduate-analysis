Exercise 3.

Let \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) and \((Y,\mathcal{N},\nu)\) be measure spaces.
🔗
  1. Let \(\mathcal{L}\) be the collection of sets \(F \subseteq X\times Y\) such that the \(x\)-section \(F_x\in \mathcal{N}\) for all \(x \in X\text{.}\) Show that \(\mathcal{L}\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra.
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Show that if \(E \subseteq X\times Y\) is in the \(\sigma\)-algebra generated by the measurable rectangles, then the \(x\)-section \(E_x \in \mathcal{N}\) for all \(x \in X\text{.}\) (HINT: Show that the measurable rectangles are in the \(\sigma\)-algebra \(\mathcal{L}\text{.}\))
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Take non-empty \(A \in \mathcal{M}\) such that \(\mu(A)=0\text{;}\) and take \(B \subseteq Y\) which is not measurable, i.e. \(B\notin \mathcal{N}\text{.}\) Show that \(A\times B\) is measurable with respect to the product measure \(\mu\times \nu\text{.}\) Show that \(A\times B\) is not in \(\mathcal{L}\text{.}\)
    🔗
