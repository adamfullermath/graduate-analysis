Exercise 5.

Let \(([0,1],\mathcal{M},m)\) be the measure space consisting of the Lebesgue measure on the Lebesgue measurable subsets of \([0,1]\text{;}\) let \(([0,1],\mathcal{M},c)\) be the measure space of consisting of the counting measure on the Lebesgue measurable subsets of \([0,1]\text{;}\) and let \(D = \{(x,x) \colon x \in [0,1]\}\text{.}\)
🔗
Show that
\begin{equation*} \int_{[0,1]\times[0,1]} \chi_D\,d(m\times c) \neq \int_{[0,1]}\left(\int_{[0,1]} \chi_D(x,y)\,dc(y)\right)dm(x). \end{equation*}
Why does this not contradict Fubini’s Theorem? Why does this not contradict Tonelli’s Theorem? (HINT: The first part of this question is essentially a rephrasing of Exercise 1.4.2.)
