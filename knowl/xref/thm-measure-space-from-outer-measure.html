Theorem 2.3.7.

Let \(X\) be a non-empty set, let \(\mu^*\) be an outer measure on \(X\text{,}\) and let \(\mathcal{M}\) be the collection of all subsets of \(X\) which are measurable with respect to \(\mu^*\text{.}\) Then \(\mathcal{M}\) is a \(\sigma\)-algebra and the restriction of \(\mu^*\) to \(\mathcal{M}\) is a measure. That is, \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) is a measure space, where \(\mu = \mu^*|_\mathcal{M}\text{.}\) Further, \((X,\mathcal{M},\mu)\) is a complete measure space.
