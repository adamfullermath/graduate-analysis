Example 4.3.11.

Let \(\mathcal{I}_1\) be the collection of all intervals in \(\mathbb{R}\text{;}\) let \(\mathcal{I}_2\) be the collection of all intervals of finite length in \(\mathbb{R}\text{;}\) and let \(\mathcal{I}_3\) be the collection of intervals of the form
🔗
\begin{equation*} \mathcal{I}_3 = \{ (a,b] \colon a \leq b\}. \end{equation*}
Then each of \(\mathcal{I}_1\text{,}\) \(\mathcal{I}_2\text{,}\) and \(\mathcal{I}_3\) are semirings of subsets of \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
