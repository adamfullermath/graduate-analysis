Proposition 2.1.3.

Let \((X,\mathcal{M})\) be a measurable space. Then
🔗
  1. \(X \in \mathcal{M}\text{;}\)
    🔗
    🔗
  2. \(\mathcal{M}\) is closed under countable intersections, i.e. if \(\{A_n\}_{n=1}^\infty\) then
    \begin{equation*} \bigcap_{n=1}^\infty A_n \in \mathcal{M}; \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
  3. \(\mathcal{M}\) is closed under finite unions and finite intersections;
    🔗
    🔗
  4. if \(A,B \in \mathcal{M}\) then \(A\backslash B \in M\text{.}\)
    🔗
    🔗
