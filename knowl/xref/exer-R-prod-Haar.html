Exercise 5.

Let \(\mu\) be the measure on \(\bR\backslash\{0\}\) defined in Example 2.2.4. Show that \(\mu\) is a Haar measure on \(\bR\backslash\{0\}\text{.}\)
🔗
Hint.
This can be done directly, by first proving the \(\mu(c(a,b]) = \mu((a,b])\) for intervals \((a,b]\subseteq \bR\) and extending the result to general Borel sets. Alternatively, it can be shown as an application of Theorem 1.3.6.
