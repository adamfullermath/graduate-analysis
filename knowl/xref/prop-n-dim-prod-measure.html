Proposition 1.1.15.

Let \(\{(X_k,\mathcal{M}_k,\mu_k)\}_{k=1}^n\) be measure spaces, let \(\mathcal{S}\) be the measurable rectangles in \(\prod_{k=1}^n X_k\text{;}\) and let \(\mu_1 \times \ldots \mu_n\) be the extended real-valued function on \(\mathcal{S}\) defined by
\begin{equation*} (\mu_1 \times \ldots \times \mu_n)(A_1\times \ldots \times A_n) = \mu_1(A_1)\mu_2(A_2)\ldots \mu_n(A_n) \end{equation*}
for \(A_k \in \mathcal{M}_k\) for \(1\leq k \leq n\text{.}\) Then \(\mathcal{S}\) is a semiring, and \(\mu_1\times\ldots\times \mu_n\) is a premeasure on \(\mathcal{S}\text{.}\)
