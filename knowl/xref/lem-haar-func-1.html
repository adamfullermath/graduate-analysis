Lemma 2.2.11.

Let \(G\) be a locally compact Hausdorff group and let \(f,g\in C_c(G)\) be non-zero positive functions. Then there exists scalars \(c_1,c_2,\ldots,c_n \gt 0\) and \(x_1,\ldots, x_n\in G\) such that
\begin{equation*} f \leq \sum_{i=1}^n c_i L_{x_i}g. \end{equation*}
🔗
🔗

Proof.

Let \(K\) be the compact support of \(f\text{,}\) and let \(U = \{x \in G\colon g(x) \gt \|g\|_\infty/2\}.\) The set \(U\) is a non-empty open set. Note that \(\{xU\}_{x\in G}\) is an open cover of \(G\text{,}\) and hence \(K\text{.}\) Since \(K\) is compact, there are \(x_1,\ldots,x_n\in G\) such that \(\{x_iU\}_{i=1}^n\) is an open cover of \(K\text{.}\) If \(x \in K\text{,}\) there exists \(y\in U\) and \(x_j\) such that \(x=x_jy\text{.}\) Since, \(\frac{2g(y)}{\|g\|_\infty}\gt 1\text{,}\) it follows that
\begin{align*} f(x) \amp \leq \|f\|_\infty\\ \amp \leq \frac{2\|f\|_\infty g(y)}{\|g\|_\infty} \\ \amp = \frac{2\|f\|_\infty}{\|g\|_\infty}L_{x_j}g(x)\\ \amp \leq \frac{2\|f\|_\infty}{\|g\|_\infty} \sum_{i=1}^n L_{x_i}g(x). \end{align*}
If \(x \notin K\text{,}\) then \(f(x) =0\) and so the inequality also holds there.
