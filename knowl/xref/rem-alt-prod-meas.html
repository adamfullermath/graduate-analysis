Remark 1.1.4.

A word of warning: Some texts define the \(\sigma\)-algebra that \(\mu\times \nu\) is defined on to be the \(\sigma\)-algebra generated by the measurable rectangles. Our definition has \(\mu\times \nu\) as a function on the \(\sigma\)-algebra of \((\mu\times\nu)^*\)-measurable sets. This may be a larger \(\sigma\)-algebra. Exercise 1.4.3 gives sufficient conditions for when the \(\sigma\)-algebra generated by measurable rectangles is smaller than \((\mu\times\nu)^*\)-measurable sets.
🔗
We discuss some differences between these different definitions of product measure further in Remark 1.1.14
