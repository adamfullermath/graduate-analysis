Exercise 12.

Let \((X,\mathcal{F})\) be a measurable space. Let \(\overline{\mathbb{R}} =\mathbb{R}\cup \{-\infty,\infty\}\) be the extended real-line and let \(\mathcal{B}(\overline{\mathbb{R}})\) be the Borel sets in \(\overline{\mathbb{R}}\text{,}\) with respect to the topology defined in Exercise 2.4.6 (see also Exercise 2.4.7).
🔗
Let \(f \colon X \rightarrow \overline{\mathbb{R}}\) be a function. Show that \(f\) is a measurable function if and only if \(f^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{F}\) for all \(B \in \mathcal{B}(\overline{\mathbb{R}})\text{.}\)
