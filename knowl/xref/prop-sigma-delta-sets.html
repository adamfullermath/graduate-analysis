Proposition 4.3.15.

Let \(\mu\) be a set function on a collection \(\mathcal{S}\) of subsets of \(X\text{,}\) and let \(\mu^*\) be the induced outer measure, and let \(\mu\) be the induced Carathéodory measure.
🔗
If \(E \subseteq X\) satisfies \(\mu^*(E)<\infty\) then there exists an \(A \in \mathcal{S}_{\sigma\delta}\) such that \(E \subseteq A\) and \(\mu^*(E) = \mu^*(A)\text{.}\) If \(E\) is \(\mu^*\)-measurable and each set in \(\mathcal{S}\) is \(\mu^*\)-measurable, then \(A\) is \(\mu^*\)-measurable and \(\mu(A \backslash E) = 0\text{.}\)
