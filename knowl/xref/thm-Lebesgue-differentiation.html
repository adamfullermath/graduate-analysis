Theorem 9.3.8. Lebesgue Differentiation Theorem.

Let \(f\) be a Lebesgue integrable function on \(\mathbb{R}\text{.}\) For each \(x \in \mathbb{R}\) and \(r>0\text{,}\) let \(E_{r^+}(x) = (x,x+r)\) and \(E_{r^-}(x) = (x-r,x)\text{.}\) Then, for almost all \(x \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\)
🔗
\begin{equation*} \lim_{r\rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{m(E_r(x))} \int_{E_r(x)} |f(y) - f(x)|\,dm(y) = 0 \end{equation*}
and
🔗
\begin{equation*} \lim_{r\rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{m(E_r(x))} \int_{E_r(x)} f\,dm = f(x) \end{equation*}
where \(E_r = E_{r^+}\text{,}\) \(E_r = E_{r^-}\) or \(E_r = B(x,r)\text{.}\)
