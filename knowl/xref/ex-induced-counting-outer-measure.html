Example 2.3.4.

Let \(X\) be an infinite set, and let \(\mathcal{S}\) be the collection of all finite subsets of \(X\text{.}\) Let \(\mu \colon \mathcal{S} \rightarrow [0,\infty)\) be the counting function. I.e. for \(A \subseteq \mathcal{S}\text{,}\) \(\mu(A)\) is the (necessarily finite) number of elements of \(A\text{.}\) If \(\mu^*\) is the outer measure induced by \(\mu\text{,}\) then \(\mu^*=c\) is the counting measure (Exercise 2.4.10).
