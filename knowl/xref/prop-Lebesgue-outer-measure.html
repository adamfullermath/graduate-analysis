Proposition 5.1.1.

Let \(\mathcal{I}\) be the semiring of all intervals in \(\mathbb{R}\text{;}\) let \(\mathcal{I}_f\) be the semiring of all finite length intervals; and let \(\mathcal{I}_h\) be the semiring of intervals of the form \((a,b]\text{,}\) where \(a,b \in \mathbb{R}\) with \(a \leq b\text{.}\) Let \(m^*\) be the outer measure induced by \(\ell\) on \(\mathcal{I}\text{;}\) let \(m_f^*\) be the outer measure induced by \(\ell\) restricted to \(\mathcal{I}_f\text{;}\) and let \(m_h^*\) be the outer measure induced by \(\ell\) restricted to \(\mathcal{I}_h\text{.}\) Then for each \(A \subseteq \mathbb{R}\)
🔗
\begin{equation*} m^*(A) = m^*_f(A) = m^*_h(A) = \inf\left\{ \sum_{i=1}^\infty \ell((a_i,b_i) \colon A \subseteq \bigcup_{i=1}^\infty (a_i,b_i)\right\}. \end{equation*}
