Exercise 13.

The proof of Young’s inequality, Lemma Lemma 10.2.12, relies on the fact that \(f(x) = e^x\) is a convex function. This will be proved in this exercise. Take \(u,v \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\)
🔗
  1. Assume that \(u \leq v\text{.}\) Show that
    \begin{equation*} e^v \geq e^u + e^u(v-u). \end{equation*}
    (HINT: First use the Mean Value Theorem to show that there is a \(w\) with \(u\leq w \leq v\) such that \(e^v=e^u +e^w(v-u)\)).
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗
  2. Assume that \(u \geq v\text{.}\) Show that
    \begin{equation*} e^v \geq e^u + e^u(v-u). \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗
  3. Take \(\lambda \in[0,1]\) and let \(w = (1-\lambda) u + \lambda v\text{.}\) Note that, by (i) and (ii),
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} e^u &\geq e^w + e^w(u-w) \\ e^v &\geq e^w + e^w(v-w). \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    Use these inequalities to show that
    🔗
    \begin{equation*} (1-\lambda)e^u + \lambda e^v \geq e^w. \end{equation*}
    That is, show that \(e^x\) is a convex function.
