Lemma 2.2.10.

Let \(G\) be a locally compact Hausdorff group. Let \(\mu\) be a Radon measure on \(G\) and let \(\phi\) be the positive linear functional on \(C_c(G)\) defined by
\begin{equation*} \phi(f) = \int_G f \,d\mu \end{equation*}
for all \(f \in C_c(G).\) The measure \(\mu\) is a left Haar measure, if and only if \(\phi\) is left invariant.
🔗
🔗

Proof.

First assume that \(\mu\) is a left Haar measure. Then, if \(B \subseteq G\) is a Borel set, and \(x \in G\text{,}\) then
\begin{equation*} \int_X L_x \chi_B\,d\mu = \int_X \chi_{xB}\,d\mu = \mu (xB) = \mu(B) = \int_X \chi_B\,d\mu. \end{equation*}
By the linearity of integration, it follows that
\begin{equation*} \int_G L_x f\,d\mu = \int_G f\,d\mu \end{equation*}
for all positive simple functions \(f\) on \(G\text{.}\) That \(\phi\) is left invariant follows by the Simple Approximation Theorem and the Monotone Convergence Theorem.
🔗
Assume now that \(\phi\) is left invariant. Fix \(U \subseteq G\) open and take any compact subset \(K \subseteq U\text{.}\) By Urysohn’s Lemma, there is an \(f \in C_c(G)\) such that \(0\leq f \leq 1\text{,}\) \(f(x) = 1\) for \(x\in K\) and \(f(x)=0\) for \(x \in G \backslash U\text{.}\) Take any \(x \in G\text{.}\) Then,
\begin{align*} \mu(xK) \amp = \int_{xK}1\,d\mu \\ \amp = \int_G L_x \chi_{K}\,d\mu \\ \amp \leq \int_G L_x f\,d\mu \\ \amp = \int_G f\,d\mu\\ \amp \leq \mu(U). \end{align*}
By the inner regularity of open sets (since \(\mu\) is a Radon measure)
\begin{align*} \mu(xU) \amp = \sup\{\mu(K) \colon K \subseteq xU \text{ compact}\} \\ \amp = \sup\{\mu(K) \colon x^{-1}K \subseteq U \text{ compact}\} \\ \amp = \sup\{\mu(xK) \colon K \subseteq U \text{ compact}\}\\ \amp \leq \mu(U). \end{align*}
A similar argument shows that \(\mu(U) \leq \mu(xU).\) We have thus shown that \(\mu(U) = \mu(xU)\) for all \(x \in G\) and \(U \subseteq G\text{.}\) It follows that \(\mu(B) = \mu(xB)\) for all \(x \in G\) and all \(B \subseteq G\) Borel. Thus, \(\mu\) is a left Haar measure.
