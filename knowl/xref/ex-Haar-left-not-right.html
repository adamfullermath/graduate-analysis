Example 2.2.7.

Let \(G\) be the semidirect product \(\bR\rtimes (0,\infty)\) with product defined as
\begin{equation*} (x,s)(y,t) = (x+sy,st), \end{equation*}
for \(x,y \in \bR\) and \(s,t \gt 0\text{.}\) Then \(G\) is a locally compact Hausdorff group (with the product topology on \(\bR \times (0,\infty)\)). As a group, \(G\) is isomorphic to the group of affine linear transformations on \(\bR\text{.}\)
🔗
Define functions \(f_L\) and \(f_R\) on \(G (= \bR\times (0,\infty)\subseteq \bR^2)\) by \(f_L(x,s)=\frac{1}{s^2}\) and \(f_R(x,s)=\frac{1}{s}.\) Define measures \(\mu_L\) and \(\mu_R\) on \(G\) by
\begin{equation*} \mu(B) = \int_B f_L\,dm_2 \text{ and } \mu_R(B) = \int_B f_R \,dm_2 \end{equation*}
for each Borel \(B \subseteq G\text{,}\) where \(m_2\) is the Lebesgue measure on \(\bR^2\) (restricted to \(G\)).
🔗
Take any Borel \(B \subseteq G\) and \((y,t)\in G\text{.}\) Then, with the aide of Tonelli’s Theorem and Theorem 1.3.6, we have
\begin{align*} \mu_L((y,t)B) \amp= \int_{(y,t)B} f_L\,dm_2 \\ \amp= t^2 \int_{\bR^2} f_L(tx,ts) \chi_{(y,t)B}(tx,ts)\,dm_2(x,s) \\ \amp= t^2 \int_{\bR}\int_{\bR} f_L(tx,ts) \chi_{(y,t)B}(tx,ts)\,dm(x)\,dm(s) \\ \amp= t^2 \int_{\bR}\int_{\bR} f_L(tx,ts) \chi_{B}((y,t)^{-1}(tx,ts))\,dm(x)\,dm(s) \\ \amp= t^2 \int_{\bR}\int_{\bR} f_L(tx,ts) \chi_{B}((t^{-1}y+x,s))\,dm(x)\,dm(s) \\ \amp= t^2 \int_{\bR}\int_{\bR} f_L(tx,ts) \chi_{B}((x,s))\,dm(x)\,dm(s) \\ \amp= t^2\int_{B} \frac{1}{(ts)^2}\,dm_2 = \mu_L(B), \end{align*}
where the third last equality apples (1.2) (from Lemma 1.3.8). Hence \(\mu_L\) is a left Haar measure. However, \(\mu_L\) is not a right Haar measure. Similarly, one can show that \(\mu_R\) is a right Haar measure, but not a left Haar measure. Details are left to Exercise 2.3.7.
